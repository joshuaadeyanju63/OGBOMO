Why Ogbomọ́

Built from Ogbomoso's own identity, not a generic template

Waste streams are named Ilé, Ọjà, and Kòtò — matching how sanitation duties are actually divided locally. Zones map to the real five local governments. The collection metaphor draws on the city's history as a walled, gated settlement, where collective vigilance was already part of civic life. This is designed to feel like it belongs to Ogbomosoland, not dropped in from outside it.

FAQ

Common questions

Is Ogbomọ́ an official government service?+

No. Ogbomọ́ is an independent prototype built to demonstrate a model for Ogbomosoland's local governments and potential partners. It is not affiliated with the Soun's palace, Oyo State, or any local government unless and until a formal partnership is agreed.

How do CleanPoints work?+

You earn points based on the weight of sorted recyclables you drop off at a hub. Once your balance crosses the redemption threshold, you can convert points to cash. Rates shown today are illustrative, pending real partner pricing.

Which areas does this cover?+

All five local governments of Ogbomosoland — Ogbomosho North, Ogbomosho South, Oriire, Ogo-Oluwa, and Surulere — though a real pilot would likely start with one before expanding.

How can my organization partner with Ogbomọ́?+

Reach out through the Contact page — we're actively looking for local government, recycling, and technology partners for a funded pilot.
