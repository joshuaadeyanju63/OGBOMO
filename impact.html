Why Ogbomosoland needs this

Ogbomosoland's waste problem is not a mystery — it is documented. This platform is built to close a gap researchers have already identified as logistical, not technological.

~745K Residents across Ogbomosoland as of 2024 — up from roughly 166,000 in 1991 and 25,000 in 1885. Growth researchers have linked directly to worsening waste management strain.
>50% Share of Ogbomoso's household waste found to be organic material in a local waste-characterization study — meaning delayed collection actively decomposes, smells, and blocks drains.
5 / 12 Local governments and LCDAs that currently coordinate sanitation separately across Ogbomosoland — a structural gap a shared dispatch board is built to bridge.
42M Tonnes of solid waste generated nationally in Nigeria each year, with researchers pointing to weak policy enforcement and low public awareness — not lack of technology — as the core failure points.
Pilot goals — not yet results

What success looks like

  • Cut average time-to-resolution on resident reports to under 24 hours
  • Give all five local governments one shared, real-time view of collection status
  • Turn resident photo reports into a verifiable evidence trail the council can act on
  • Produce the route and volume data needed to plan staffing and truck allocation with evidence, not guesswork
  • Establish at least one working connection between sorted household recyclables and a paying buyer
Sources

Peer-reviewed studies on Ogbomoso solid waste generation and composition (2015–2021); Ogbomosho South local government waste-logistics research; national solid waste statistics from Nigerian waste policy literature; Ogbomosho population data (Wikipedia, 2024 estimate). Figures are drawn from independent academic research, not official council statistics, and are presented as the basis for a pilot, not a completed deployment.
