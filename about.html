Ìtàn · Our Story

Waste isn't just an environmental problem — it's a design problem

Ogbomọ́ started with a simple observation: recyclable materials are thrown away, households lack simple sorting systems, and young people are shut out of an economy that already exists around them.

Where this began

An architecture student's observation

This platform began with an architecture student studying sustainable development and circular economy principles, who noticed the same pattern repeating across Ogbomosoland: valuable materials — plastic, metal, paper — treated as rubbish instead of resources, households with no simple way to separate what they throw away, and young people with no clear path into the recycling economy already forming informally around them.

That observation became the starting point for Ogbomọ́ — not as a waste collection app, but as infrastructure for a different relationship with waste altogether.

Mission

Transform waste into economic opportunity while building cleaner, smarter, more sustainable communities.

Vision

To become one of Africa's leading digital circular economy platforms — connecting citizens, collectors, recyclers, businesses, schools, and local government in one system.

The Problem, In Full

What we're actually solving for

Poor collection

Irregular municipal pickup leaves waste sitting, decomposing, and blocking drains.

Low participation

Recycling exists informally, but with no easy on-ramp for most households.

Poor segregation

Without a simple sorting system, recyclable material ends up landfilled with everything else.

Illegal dumping

Roadsides, drains, and open spaces absorb waste that should have a real destination.

Limited education

Few residents have been taught what's recyclable, or why it matters.

Weak incentives

There's little reason to sort waste carefully if nothing comes back for the effort.

Youth unemployment

The recycling value chain could employ far more young people than it currently does.

Environmental pressure

Pollution and resource strain grow with the city, unless something changes the trajectory.

Who this is for

Built for everyone touching the waste system

Households and students. Youth looking for income. Waste collectors and recycling companies. Schools and businesses. NGOs, government agencies, and investors who want to back a model that actually works before scaling it further.
